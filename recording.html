Parts of Prayer | Praise

1. What does it mean to praise the Lord?

2. How do we praise the Lord?

3. How should we praise the Lord?

Homework

1. Read Psalms 145. While reading the verses write down the things you see that God is and what He has done and what He does. Basically look for the things in the verses that you can praise God for.

2. After writing down the things you can praise God for, in your praying time, use those things in your prayer when you are praising the Lord. You will repeat this process until you have finished the chapter, then we will check your work in the next lesson. NOTE: you will not get graded for this! Just do your best.

Lastly

I also want to clarify some things regarding the lesson, that I forgot mention.

1. When I said that God judges people that put him in the place other than first by death. I was meaning that that is what he used in the Old Testament. Yes, God can use death as a last resort to try to get the person back on track, but I was not meaning that if you don't put Him first, he will immediately kill you! Sorry I didn't clarify. I mean, I think my Dad knew what I was meaning, but he's my dad, and I forgot to mention some things!

2. When I was saying that if you put him first, and everything will fall into there places, I was meaning that everything else will get in there right place of priority.

3. When I said don't use repetition, I was meaning don't just pray in vain the same old prayer over and over again, but pray from the heart.
