Lesson 30 2nd Meeting

Thinking About the Lord

1. What do you think about God? When you think about God, what comes to mind?

2. Who is God to you? What does God mean to you personally? Seriously, what does God mean to you? Is He simply someone you have heard about, or is He, the Lord God, everything to you?

3. How often do you think about the Lord? Once a year? Once a month? Once a week? Once a day? Or do you constantly think and meditate on the Lord throughout your day? Now, how often do you think the Lord thinks about you? Once a year? Once a month? Once a week? Once a day? How often? The Lord is always thinking about you. His thoughts for you number more than the sand in the sea. He thinks about you all the time!

4. What if the Lord wasn't _____? What if He didn't have _____? What if the Lord wasn't long suffering? Think about that! If the Lord wasn't long suffering, we would all be dead! Time after time we make a mistake, and time after time God forgives and gives us another chance. Ask yourself those two questions and fill in the blank, and think about it. If one God's characters were missing, or if He didn't have that piece of character, it would affect everything else. Thank the Lord that He is _____, or that He has ____. We take God's character for granted, because we know God's character isn't going to change, so we don't need to worry about it, and just move on. But stop and think, and thank and praise the Lord that He is _____ or that He has ______.

If you want to learn about God, and know Him, then read the Bible. The book of Psalms is a great place to learn about God's character, because it is packed full of Psalms about the Lord. If you are having a hard time praising the Lord, read the Psalms. Go through and pray through a Psalm. It works! The reason why I know what I know about the Lord, is because I read through the Psalms, and I am still reading the Psalms and learning more!

Here is my challenge for you guys: Learn about the Lord. Think and meditate on Him and His word. It's great to think about the Lord, and we should think about the Lord, and we should do it often, throughout the day. Ask yourself these questions. Ask yourself questions about the Lord. Think and meditate on Him. I personally love thinking and meditating on the Lord. For one, it encourages me. Two, it humbles me! When I think about the Lord and how great He is, I feel like I don't know anything, and I feel like I am powerless (because I am powerless!). I just feel so small compared to the Lord! When you wake up in the morning start praising the Lord! When I wake up in the morning, the first thing I think about is almost always the Lord. I wake up, and I say, "Good morning Lord!", or "Good morning Dad!" The first and last thing I try to think about in my day, is the Lord.

Who/What are you going to follow? Who/What are you going to love? Who/What are you going to serve? God or mammon (riches, the world, Satan, self)? Are you ready to to do what ever God asks you to do? Are you ready to go wherever the Lord asks you to go? Are you ready to die for the Lord? Are you going to pick up your cross and follow Jesus? Or are you going to follow after this world and yourself? I recommend following the Lord.

Spend time with the Lord. Spend time with the Lord! I can't make you spend time with the Lord! I can say it all I want, but I can't make you follow and spend time with the Lord. You have to make the decision to follow after God. YOU have start by saying, "I will..." I starts by making a decision to follow the Lord no matter what. Only you can make that decision, and I pray you do.

So, when are you going to start? Ten years from now? Or are you going to start now?

Someone After God's Own Heart

1. Someone After God's own heart is someone who follows after, or pursues after God

2. Someone After God's own heart is someone who worships God

3. Someone After God's own heart is someone who knows Gods Heart. His loves, hates, and desires. Not only knowing, but making His loves your loves, His hates your hates, and His desires your desires.

4. Someone After God's own heart is someone who takes responsibility for their wrong doing and confesses their sins.

5. Someone After God's own heart is someone who loves God's word

6. Someone After God's own heart is someone who humble

Being someone after God's own heart does not mean that we will be perfect and not make any mistakes. We will still make mistakes.

Lesson 22

An Addition Or A Substitution?

Don't make God an addition to your life, make Him your life. You can't serve two masters, you will hate one and love the other. You will love God or you will love riches. Are you loving God, or are you loving riches and this world? Can't love and serve both. When we were saved, the Old man died. We are a new creation. We have put on the new man, so we should do what the new man would do, not the old man. The old man will love the riches of this world, but the new man will love God.

Matthew 6:24 - "No man can serve two masters: for either he will hate the one, and love the other; or else he will hold to the one, and despise the other. Ye cannot serve God and mammon."

Colossians 3:5-17 - "Mortify therefore your members which are upon the earth; fornication, uncleanness, inordinate affection, evil concupiscence, and covetousness, which is idolatry: For which things' sake the wrath of God cometh on the children of disobedience: In the which ye also walked some time, when ye lived in them. But now ye also put off all these; anger, wrath, malice, blasphemy, filthy communication out of your mouth. Lie not one to another, seeing that ye have put off the old man with his deeds; And have put on the new man, which is renewed in knowledge after the image of him that created him: Where there is neither Greek nor Jew, circumcision nor uncircumcision, Barbarian, Scythian, bond nor free: but Christ is all, and in all. Put on therefore, as the elect of God, holy and beloved, bowels of mercies, kindness, humbleness of mind, meekness, longsuffering; Forbearing one another, and forgiving one another, if any man have a quarrel against any: even as Christ forgave you, so also do ye. And above all these things put on charity, which is the bond of perfectness. And let the peace of God rule in your hearts, to the which also ye are called in one body; and be ye thankful. Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom; teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts to the Lord. And whatsoever ye do in word or deed, do all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God and the Father by him."

Plan your battle to win

If we are to be true soldiers for Christ, we need to share the gospel with someone or at least give them a track.
