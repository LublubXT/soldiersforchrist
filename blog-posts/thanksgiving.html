Happy Thanksgiving!

Published 11/24/22

Written by Caleb J. Gross

What is Thanksgiving?

Thanksgiving is a time each year that family and friends come together to thank God for His wonderful provision and protection, eat special meals, read from God's word, play games, and talk. Thanksgiving is a time, day, and holiday that we all look forward to each year. It is a time to relax and enjoy eating good food.

When was the first Thanksgiving?

In the month of November 1621, after one of the first successful harvests, William Bradford, the governor of Plymouth, set up a feast to celebrate the successful harvest. They also invited the Wampanoag Indians to join them in the feast. Although this was the “first” thanksgiving, it was not declared a holiday till about two-hundred forty years later.

When was Thanksgiving a holiday?

Thanksgiving finally became a holiday in 1863 during the Civil war. It was announced by Abraham Lincoln, that thanksgiving was to become a national holiday, and was to take place every November.

What did the colonists eat and do for Thanksgiving?

The colonists ate what we would call, thanksgiving food like turkey (they did not eat turkey for the first one), venison (most of us tend to use ham instead), and vegetables. I don’t know if they ate mashed potatoes, and I am pretty sure they weren’t eating sweet potato casserole with marshmallows on top! Aside from thanking God for the bountiful harvest, they played ball games (maybe soccer or the medieval version of baseball), sang, and danced. How we got to football being the popular ball game instead of baseball, I don’t know!

Fun facts about Thanksgiving

Abraham Lincoln proclaimed Thanksgiving a national holiday on October 3, 1863. Sarah Josepha Hale, the woman who wrote “Mary Had A Little Lamb,” convinced Lincoln to make Thanksgiving a national holiday after writing letters for 17 years.

The history of U.S. presidents pardoning turkeys is patchy. Harry Truman is often credited with being the first president to pardon a turkey, but that’s not quite true. He was the first to receive a ceremonial turkey from the National Turkey Federation – and he had it for dinner. John F. Kennedy was the first to let a Thanksgiving turkey go, followed by Richard Nixon who sent his turkey to a petting zoo. George H.W. Bush is the president who formalized the turkey pardoning tradition in 1989.

There are four towns in the United States named “Turkey.” They can be found in Arizona, Texas, Louisiana, and North Carolina.

The average number of calories consumed on Thanksgiving is 4,500.

The tradition of football on Thanksgiving began in 1876 with a game between Yale and Princeton. The first NFL games were played on Thanksgiving in 1920.

More than 54 million Americans are expected to travel during the Thanksgiving holiday this year. That’s up 4.8% from last year.
